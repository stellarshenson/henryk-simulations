# Testimony - Victim

## Victoria's perceived recollection of the event

Victoria's account has been recorded multiple times since the day of the event. The accounts diverge in detail and escalate over time.

### As exclaimed at the moment (live audio recording)

At the moment of the contested incident Victoria screamed: *"He threw himself at me at the neck!"*

### As reported to the examining physician (two days after the event)

Victoria states she was pushed by Andrew against the elevator door, struck the door with her back, and fell to the floor striking her buttocks. She reports no other injuries and no loss of consciousness.

### As described in the earliest legal filing (motion to the prosecutor)

*Verbatim translation of the relevant sentence:* "Andrew committed a brutal physical assault on Victoria, throwing himself at her and pushing her, the result of which was bodily injury."

No throat-grab, no strangulation attempt, and no left-side approach are mentioned in this earliest legal-filing version. The mechanism is described as Andrew throwing himself at Victoria and pushing her.

### As described in a later legal filing

Andrew rushed at Victoria and pushed her. Andrew attempted to grab Victoria by the throat. In self-defense, Victoria grabbed Andrew with both hands to prevent him from grabbing her throat. Andrew then threw Victoria against the elevator door, opposite the apartment entrance. The impact against the elevator door was so strong that Victoria fell to the ground. Victoria screamed "help!" and was crying from fear and pain.

### As described in a still-later legal filing

Andrew attempted to strangle Victoria and then violently threw her against the elevator door, causing the bodily injuries documented in the forensic medical examination. Andrew approached Victoria from her left side; Victoria has documented partial blindness in her left eye, of which Andrew is aware. Victoria characterises this as a premeditated attack from her "blind spot" that prevented her from avoiding the grip on her throat or mounting any defence in the first phase of the assault.

## Examination findings

- Bruising of the skin of the right shoulder extending onto the arm, approximately 12 cm × 6 cm
- Edema and moderate pain-limited range of motion in the right shoulder joint
- X-ray of the right shoulder: no bony injuries
- Forward flexion of the neck full and unrestricted; right-left rotation full with mild pain in the cervical spine
- Reported symptoms: shoulder pain radiating to the right arm and forearm with numbness of the fingers of the right hand, and headache
