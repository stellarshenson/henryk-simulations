# Victoria's Testimony - Evolution Across Five Tellings

Victoria's account of the contested moment has been recorded five times since the day of the event. The mechanism described changes with each retelling. The table below lists the versions in chronological order.

| # | Source | Mechanism described |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Live audio (the moment) | "He threw himself at me at the neck" |
| 2 | Medical examination (two days later) | "Pushed against the elevator door, struck it with my back, fell on buttocks" |
| 3 | Earliest legal filing (October motion to the prosecutor) | "Threw himself at her and pushed her" - no throat-grab, no strangulation, no left-side detail |
| 4 | Later legal filing (December motion) | "Tried to grab my throat → I defensively grabbed his hands → he threw me against the door" |
| 5 | Still-later legal filing (March motion) | "Attempted to strangle + violently threw against the door + attack from her left blind side" |

## Notes on the progression

- **Versions 1 and 3** (live audio and earliest legal filing) describe a single mechanism: "threw himself at me / her". No throat contact, no defensive grab, no strangulation, no left-side approach.
- **Version 2** (medical examination) introduces the elevator-door impact and the back-first orientation. The mechanism becomes "pushed", not "threw himself".
- **Version 4** (December filing) introduces three new elements simultaneously: a throat-grab attempt, Victoria's defensive grab of Andrew's hands, and a throw against the door.
- **Version 5** (March filing) escalates the throat-grab attempt into an attempted strangulation, adds a "violently" qualifier to the throw, and adds the new element of an attack from Victoria's blind left side.

Each subsequent telling adds elements not present in earlier tellings. None of the later additions (throat-grab, defensive hand-grab, strangulation attempt, blind-left-side approach) appears in the contemporaneous record (versions 1 and 2) or in the earliest legal filing (version 3).
