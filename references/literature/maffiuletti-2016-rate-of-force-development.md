# Maffiuletti et al. (2016) - Rate of Force Development Review

Maffiuletti, N.A., Aagaard, P., Blazevich, A.J., Folland, J., Tillin, N. & Duchateau, J. (2016). Rate of force development: physiological and methodological considerations. *European Journal of Applied Physiology*, 116(6), 1091-1116. DOI: 10.1007/s00421-016-3346-6

**Access**: Open access on PubMed Central (PMC4875063, full text at https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4875063/). The PMC deposit is HTML/XML only - no publisher PDF is available, so no PDF copy is held locally.

## What it establishes

Review of the physiology and measurement of the rate of force development.

## Values used in this project

Early-phase RFD (first 50-75 ms) is governed by motor-unit recruitment and discharge rate; force in the first 40 ms of a rapid contraction is about 60% below the tetanic value; athletic explosive tasks have contraction times of 50-250 ms. Used to floor the start-ramp give at about 0.12 s.

## Where it is used

Exclusion-zone `tau_give` floor (0.12 s) and jerk ceiling in notebook 01b.
