# Aagaard et al. (2002) - Rate of Force Development

Aagaard, P., Simonsen, E.B., Andersen, J.L., Magnusson, P. & Dyhre-Poulsen, P. (2002). Increased rate of force development and neural drive of human skeletal muscle following resistance training. *Journal of Applied Physiology*, 93(4), 1318-1326. DOI: 10.1152/japplphysiol.00283.2002

**Access**: Free legacy content (American Physiological Society), DOI 10.1152/japplphysiol.00283.2002. PDF held locally: `aagaard-2002-rate-of-force-development.pdf`.

## What it establishes

Contractile rate of force development of the knee extensors, measured over fixed time windows from contraction onset, and its increase after training.

## Values used in this project

Contractile RFD over 30, 50, 100 and 200 ms windows: roughly 1,100-2,200 N m s^-1, rising 25-33% after 14 weeks of heavy-resistance training. Explosive voluntary force develops progressively, not instantaneously.

## Where it is used

Exclusion-zone start-ramp `tau_give` floor and jerk ceiling in notebook 01b.
